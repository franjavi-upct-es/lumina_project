    Cross-Modal Attention and DeepFusionNexus¶

    Relevant source files
    • backend/api/schemas.py
    • backend/fusion/cross_attention.py
    • backend/fusion/nexus.py
    • backend/fusion/parity_check.py
    • notebooks/05_fusion_parity_check.ipynb

    The DeepFusionNexus (often referred to as the "Thamalus" of the Chimera architecture) is the second layer of the Lumina V3 system backend/fusion/nexus.py#2 It is responsible for integrating three disparate modality embeddings, Temporal (Price), Semantic (News), and Structural (Graph), into a unified 256-dimensional latent state that serves as the observation vector for the RL Agent backend/fusion/nexus.py#6-13

    Architectural Overview¶

    The Nexus operates on a "Super-Vector" approach. Instead of projecting all modalities to a shared dimension first, the raw embeddings are concatenated to preserve their specific information capacities before being processed by a Cross-Modal Attention block backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#6-11

    Data Flow and Dimensionality¶

    The fusion process follows a strict dimensional contract:

    1. Input: Price (128-d) + Semantic (64-d) + Graph (32-d) = 224-d Super-Vector backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#11
    2. Attention: Multi-head self-attention re-weights the streams backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#13
    3. Gating: A learned sigmoid mask scales the re-weighted vector backend/fusion/nexus.py#58-61
    4. Projection: A deepened MLP projects the 224-d vector to the final 256-d Latent State backend/fusion/nexus.py#63-73

    Cross-Modal Attention (Tsai et al. 2019)¶

    The CrossModalAttention block implements a variation of the Multimodal Transformer backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#35-36 Because the input dimensions differ (128, 64, 32), the block uses interanl "read" and "write" projections to allow a standard Transformer encoder to operate on the streams as a sequence of three tokens backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#19-23

    Implementation Details¶

    • Read Projections: Linear layers (read_price, read_semantic, read_graph) project each modality to a shared d_attn space (default 512) backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#83-86
    • Modality Embeddings: A learned parameter modality_embed is added to the tokens to identify the source stream (Price vs. News vs. Graph) backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#112
    • Write Projections: After passing through nn.TransformerEncoderLayer backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#96 tokens are projected back to their native dimensions backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#89-91
    • Residual Connection: The re-weighted output is added to the original input embeddings (native-space residual) backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#170-172

    Sources: backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#49-118

    Gating and Uncertainty Estimation¶

    Sigmoid Gating Mechanism¶

    After attention, the Nexus applies a learned soft mask. The gate is a linear layer followed by a Sigmoid activation backend/fusion/nexus.py#58-61 It produces values in \((0,1)\) that are used for residual gating:

    \[ \text{output}=\text{fused}\times (1.0+\text{gate}) \]

    This ensures that the model can up-weight critical regions of the super-vector without the risk of the signal vanishing. backend/fusion/nexus.py#98-99

    MC-Dropout Uncertainty¶

    The Nexus provides a method encode_with_uncertainty to estimate epistemic uncertainty backend/fusion/nexus.py#110

    1. It triggers \(N\) stochastic forward passes (default MC_DROPOUT_SAMPLES) with dropout enabled backend/fusion/nexus.py#126-131
    2. It calculates the mean and std across these samples backend/fusion/nexus.py#133
    3. The std represents the fusion-level uncertainty, which the StateAssembler uses to trigger the agent's Uncertainty Gate backend/fusion/nexus.py#18-21

    Sources: backend/fusion/nexus.py#110-133

    Training and Parity Check¶

    Nexus Regime Warmup¶

    The Nexus weights are given a supervised "warmup" via the DailyNexusDemoModel wrapper backend/simulation/article_simulation.py#180-192 which attaches an auxiliary regime_head to train the Nexus to classify market regimes (Crash, Bear, Flat, Bull) based on the signatures in the super-vector backend/simulation/article_simulation.py#61 Driven by train_daily_nexus backend/simulation/article_simulation.py#521 this ensures the RL agent receives a stable representation from the start of its training.

    Parity Check (V2 vs V3)¶

    A parity check is conducted to ensure the Chimera (V3) architecture outperforms the simple MLP/LSTM baseline (V2) notebooks/05_fusion_parity_check.ipynb

    • Criterion: V3 Sharpe Ratio \(\ge\) V2 Sharpe Ratio on held-out data notebooks/05_fusion_parity_check.ipynb
    • Regime Signatures: Synthetic data is generated where specific regimes have unique biases in one modality (e.g., "Crash" is dominated by the Graph signal) notebooks/05_fusion_parity_check.ipynb
    • Results: The Nexus converges over 50 epochs, achieving lower MSE loss and higher regime classification accuracy than the baseline notebooks/05_fusion_parity_check.ipynb

    Sources: notebooks/05_fusion_parity_check.ipynb backend/fusion/parity_check.py#99-185
