    Fusion Layer: Deep Fusion Nexus¶

    Relevant source files
    • backend/data_engine/storage/timescale.py
    • backend/fusion/cross_attention.py
    • backend/fusion/nexus.py
    • backend/fusion/state_assembler.py
    • notebooks/05_fusion_parity_check.ipynb

    The Fusion Layer (Layer 2) serves as the "Thalamus" of the Lumina V3 architecture. Its primary role is to ingest the high-dimensional embeddings produced by the three modality-specific encoders and synthesize them into a unified, 256-dimensional Latent State Representation. This fused state provides the Cognition Layer: RL Agent and Training with a coherent view of the market, resolving conflicts between price action, news sentiment, and structural supply-chain pressures.

    The core of this layer is the DeppFusionNexus backend/fusion/nexus.py#37-48 which orchestrates cross-modal attention, gating, and latent projection.

    Architectural Overview¶

    The fusion process follows a strict "Super-Vector" concatenation strategy. Rather than projecting embeddings to a shared dimension immediately, the system preserves the information capacity of each modality (Price: 128-d, Semantic: 64-d, Structural: 32-d) by raw-concatenating them into a 224-d vector backend/fusion/cross_attention.py

    Key Components¶

    1. Cross-Modal Attention (DeepFusionNexus)¶

    The CrossModalAttention block implements a multi-head self-attention mechanism where each modality is treated as a separate "token" backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#25-31 This allows the model to dynamically re-weight stream; for instance, prioritizing the Semantic (news) stream during an earnings call while down-weighting Price noise backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#28-30

    • Mechanism: Uses per-modality "read" and "write" projections to manage dimension mismatches inside the attention space backend/fusion/cross_attention.py#19-22
    • Gating: A sigmoid gate self.gate allows the model to up- or two down-weight specific regions of the super-vector backend/fusion/nexus.py#58-61
    • Uncertainty: The Nexus uses MC-Dropout to estimate epistemic uncertainty by running multiple stochastic forward passes backend/fusion/nexus.py#110-123

    For details on the mathematical implementation and the training parity check, see Cross-Modal Attention and DeepFusionNexus.

    2. State Assembler¶

    The StateAssembler is the operational service that bridges the Feature Store and the Cognition Layer. It operates in two distinct modes:

    • Live Mode: Consumes embeddings from Redis at a 1-Hz cadence and publishes the 256-d state back to Redis backend/fusion/state_assembler.py#108-110
    • Arena Mode: Wires encoders (TemporalFusionTransformer, DistilledFinancialEncoder, GraphEncoder) directly to the Nexus for inline execution during simulations backend/fusion/state_assembler.py#146-170

    The StateAssembler also captures RawAttributionTensors (byproducts of the forward pass like cross_modal_weights and vsn_weights_by_feature) which are used for Explainable AI (XAI) backend/fusion/state_assembler.py#40-66

    For details on the service lifecycle and Redis integration, see State Assembler.
